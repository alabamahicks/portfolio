About Me

Hi, I'm

Sarah!

Introduction

I'm a front-end developer, specializing in inventory-management applications for the last 3 years. That work centered around iOS devices until this year, when our small team was asked to 'pinch-hit', shipping a mobile-ready web app on an aggressive schedule. So I'm rediscovering web dev and remembering how engrossing CSS is!

I'm constantly honing my skills - I'm always aiming to be to be relevant, competent, reliable, and fast! I love helping businesses design and build systems that get the right job done.

Where I'm From

I've lived in the American Southeast most of my life, but have moved around between beach and mountains, big city and farmland.

More About Me

What are your favorite hobbies?

Great food is a big part of my life, and I prefer to find it in beautiful surroundings. Failing the view, I'll settle into a gorgeous movie. I keep "computer crone" syndrome away with yoga practice and frequent floor sitting.

What's your dream job?

My dream job allows me to use my design education and experience from my architect's practice to build beautiful digital systems with a strategic eye on the big business picture, and to keep up with state-of-the-art tech. I get to identify needs and requirements, analyze priorities, build implementations and plan reuse....and play with CSS!

What music have you been listening to lately?

Projects

2015-09-10

Portfolio

I enrolled in Thinkful's Front End Development course largely due to this project: because I work full-time on corporate web apps, I needed to feel accountable to a structure that would work for me, and be guaranteed to yield my own valuable assets.

2015-08-29

Clone Karma Homepage

Since an essential part of a web developer's job is replicating a visual, this assignment from Thinkful's Front End Development course required copying the home page used by the startup Karma. This activity practiced more complex layout ideas and introduced further web development resources. Also at this point in the course, we began the habit of sharing work progress via GitHub.

2015-08-11

Hack the NYT

Part of the Front End Development course offered by Thinkful, this exercise required using in-browser developer tools to "hack", or reverse-engineer a complex working site, in order to experiment with well-structured, terse code. I learned how powerful small changes can be on the New York Times' site...for example, the mustard areas were all created by one change, in one place.

2015-09-07

Responsive About Me Page

This first exercise in the Thinkful's Front End Development course began to break down some out-of-date habits I brought to the course, and familiarize me with current semantic layout and best practices. In the course, we circled back and made it responsive to small screens, which is a frequent requirement.
